## 03. Realized Volatility (Part 1)

— **Anton Kriel**

---

## Why Realized Volatility Matters

Most traders talk about **direction**.  
Professionals care first about **volatility**.

Volatility determines:

- Position size
    
- Risk exposure
    
- Drawdowns
    
- Strategy viability
    
- Whether a system survives at all
    

If you misunderstand volatility, **everything built on top of it is wrong** — even if your signals are “correct.”

---

## Volatility Is Not Prediction — It Is Measurement

A core mistake retail traders make is treating volatility as:

- A forecast
    
- A forward-looking belief
    
- A directional signal
    

**Realized volatility** is none of those.

It is:

- A **measurement of what actually happened**
    
- A statistical summary of price movement magnitude
    
- Direction-agnostic
    
- Backward-looking by definition
    

Professionals separate:

- **What happened** (realized)
    
- **What might happen** (implied / assumed)
    

Confusing these leads to systematic mis-sizing.

---

## What Realized Volatility Actually Is

At its core, realized volatility measures:

- The **dispersion of returns**
    
- Over a specified time window
    
- Using observed price changes
    

Key properties:

- It ignores direction
    
- Large up and down moves both increase volatility
    
- Quiet trends can have low volatility
    
- Choppy markets can have high volatility even if price goes nowhere
    

Volatility is about **movement**, not profit.

---

## Why Price Alone Is a Lie

Two markets can:

- End at the same price
    
- Produce radically different trading outcomes
    

Example:

- Smooth trend upward → low volatility
    
- Violent up/down swings → high volatility
    

Retail intuition sees “same result.”  
Professionals see **completely different risk environments**.

Volatility tells you:

- How violent the path was
    
- How hard it was to stay in the trade
    
- Whether leverage would have killed you
    
- Whether drawdowns were survivable
    

---

## The Path Matters More Than the Destination

Retail thinking:

- Entry + exit defines success
    

Professional thinking:

- The **entire path** defines survivability
    

Even if a trade ends profitably:

- Large interim drawdowns may have forced liquidation
    
- Margin calls may have occurred
    
- Risk rules may have been violated
    

Realized volatility captures **path risk**, not just endpoints.

---

## Volatility Clusters — It Is Not Independent

One of the most important observations:

> Volatility clusters.

This means:

- High volatility tends to follow high volatility
    
- Low volatility tends to persist
    
- Calm markets can suddenly transition into violent regimes
    

Volatility is **autocorrelated**, even when returns are not.

Retail traders assume:

- Yesterday doesn’t matter
    

Professionals know:

- Volatility regimes persist
    
- Risk today depends on risk yesterday
    

---

## Why This Breaks Retail Position Sizing

Retail traders often size positions:

- Based on confidence
    
- Based on signal strength
    
- Based on account balance alone
    

Professionals size positions based on:

- Current realized volatility
    
- Worst-case volatility expansion
    
- Drawdown tolerance
    
- Regime assumptions
    

Same trade idea, different volatility → **different position size**.

Ignoring this is how traders blow up during regime shifts.

---

## Volatility Expansion Is the Killer

The real danger is not high volatility itself.

It is:

- **Unexpected volatility expansion**
    

Markets often:

- Appear calm
    
- Encourage leverage
    
- Then violently reprice
    

Strategies that work in low volatility:

- Become lethal in high volatility
    
- Especially when leverage is static
    

Professionals assume volatility will expand **before** it does.

Retail traders discover it **after** the damage.

---

## Why Fixed Stops and Fixed Sizes Fail

Using:

- Fixed stop distances
    
- Fixed position sizes
    

implicitly assumes:

- Volatility is stable
    

It is not.

Professionals:

- Scale position size inversely with volatility
    
- Accept smaller profits during volatile periods
    
- Trade larger during stable periods
    

The goal is **risk consistency**, not profit consistency.

---

## Realized Volatility as a Risk Control Tool

Professionals use realized volatility to:

- Normalize risk across trades
    
- Compare instruments fairly
    
- Adjust leverage dynamically
    
- Detect regime changes early
    
- Avoid false confidence during calm periods
    

It is not a signal generator.  
It is a **risk thermostat**.

---

## Why Retail Charts Hide Volatility

Candlestick charts:

- Emphasize direction
    
- Hide path violence
    
- Encourage narrative interpretation
    

Volatility statistics:

- Strip narrative
    
- Expose risk reality
    
- Reveal fragility
    

Professionals trust numbers over pictures.

---

## Key Takeaways (Expanded)

- Volatility measures **movement**, not direction
    
- Realized volatility is backward-looking but essential
    
- Path risk matters more than endpoints
    
- Volatility clusters into regimes
    
- Volatility expansion causes most blow-ups
    
- Position sizing must adapt to volatility
    
- Risk consistency beats profit consistency

## 1. Why China Matters (Even If You Don’t Trade China)

Retail traders often think:

> “China is a separate market.”

Professionals know:

- China sits at the **center of global production**
    
- China is upstream of commodities, manufacturing, and trade
    
- Stress in China propagates **outward**, not inward
    

China is not a regional signal.  
It is a **global supply-chain and demand signal**.

---

## 2. What These PMIs Actually Measure

China has **two manufacturing PMI surveys**, each capturing a different slice of reality:

### Official PMI

- Focused on large, state-owned enterprises
    
- Heavily influenced by government policy
    
- Reflects infrastructure, heavy industry, state priorities
    

### Caixin PMI

- Focused on small- and mid-sized private firms
    
- Export-oriented
    
- More sensitive to global demand and credit conditions
    

Professionals track **both**, because divergence is informative.

---

## 3. Why PMI Data Is Leading (Again)

Like ISM:

- PMIs are survey-based
    
- They measure **expectations and activity**
    
- They move _before_ hard data
    

Manufacturing PMIs reflect:

- New orders
    
- Production intent
    
- Export demand
    
- Inventory behavior
    

That makes them **early indicators of stress or recovery**.

---

## 4. Why China PMIs Are Systemically Important

China sits upstream of:

- Global manufacturing
    
- Commodities
    
- Shipping and logistics
    
- Emerging market growth
    
- Industrial demand
    

When China manufacturing weakens:

- Commodity demand falls
    
- Export economies feel stress
    
- Global growth assumptions weaken
    
- Risk assets become more fragile
    

This is not about China equities.  
It’s about **global transmission**.

---

## 5. Official vs Caixin: How Professionals Interpret Divergence

Divergence is common — and useful.

Examples:

- **Official strong, Caixin weak**  
    → State support masking private-sector stress
    
- **Caixin strong, Official weak**  
    → Export demand improving before domestic recovery
    
- **Both weakening**  
    → Broad industrial slowdown, global risk rising
    

Professionals care less about the number and more about:

- Direction
    
- Persistence
    
- Agreement or disagreement
    

---

## 6. The 50 Line (Again, Context > Precision)

As with ISM:

- Above 50 → expansion
    
- Below 50 → contraction
    

But professionals:

- Do not trade crossings
    
- Do not react to single prints
    
- Track sustained trends
    

A persistent move below 50 in both PMIs:

- Signals systemic industrial weakness
    
- Raises global fragility
    
- Tightens risk posture

## 7. China PMIs as a Global Confirmation Layer

Anton’s implicit framing:

> China PMIs do not _cause_ global stress —  
> they **confirm whether it is spreading through production**.

When China PMIs weaken alongside:

- Credit stress
    
- Tightening liquidity
    
- Weakening ISM
    
- European sentiment deterioration
    

That alignment signals **late-cycle or hostile global regimes**.

## 8. Why Retail Traders Misuse China Data

Retail mistakes include:

- Ignoring China entirely
    
- Treating it as a “China trade”
    
- Overreacting to policy headlines
    
- Assuming government support eliminates risk
    

Professionals assume:

- Policy can delay pain
    
- It cannot remove constraints
    
- Weak PMIs mean fragile supply chains
    

---

## 9. What This Indicator Is _Not_

China PMIs are **not**:

- Timing tools
    
- Trade triggers
    
- Signals for FX or equities
    

They are:

- **Context**
    
- **Confirmation**
    
- **Risk amplifiers**
    

Exactly like ESI — but upstream in production.

### Rule B — Persistence Requirement

- Weakness persists
    
- Directional trend is clear
    
- Both surveys confirm _or_ divergence is economically meaningful
    

No reaction to noise.

China PMIs **accelerate escalation**, not trigger it.

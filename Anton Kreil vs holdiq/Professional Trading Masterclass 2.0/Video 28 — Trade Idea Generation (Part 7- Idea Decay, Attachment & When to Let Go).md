## What This Lecture Is Really About

This lecture answers a subtle but critical question:

> _“Why do traders keep trading ideas that are no longer valid — even when they know better?”_

The answer is **idea attachment**.

Most traders do not fail because they lack ideas.  
They fail because they **cannot let go of old ones**.

---

## The Core Reality: All Edges Decay

Anton makes this explicit:

> **No edge is permanent.**

Edges decay because:

- Capital flows adapt
    
- Crowds learn
    
- Conditions change
    
- Constraints loosen or tighten
    
- Regimes shift
    

An idea that worked brilliantly:

- Can slowly weaken
    
- Can stop working quietly
    
- Can then fail catastrophically if forced
    

Professionals assume **decay is inevitable**.

---

## Why Traders Get Attached to Ideas

Attachment forms because:

- The idea made money before
    
- The trader invested time and ego
    
- The narrative still sounds right
    
- Losses feel “temporary”
    

This leads to dangerous thoughts:

- “It owes me”
    
- “It worked last year”
    
- “The logic hasn’t changed”
    
- “It’s just unlucky”
    

Markets do not care about effort or logic.

---

## Idea Decay Is Usually Gradual, Not Sudden

A key insight:

> **Most edges do not disappear overnight — they erode.**

Signs of decay include:

- Lower hit rates
    
- Smaller winners
    
- Longer holding times
    
- Higher volatility
    
- Increased drawdowns
    

Retail traders ignore these and wait for a dramatic failure.

Professionals detect **early degradation** and respond before disaster.

---

## The Professional Rule: Ideas Must Earn Their Keep Continuously

Anton reframes idea ownership:

> **Ideas are not assets.  
> They are liabilities that must justify their existence every day.**

Professionals require:

- Ongoing performance validation
    
- Regime consistency
    
- Risk-adjusted contribution
    
- Portfolio benefit
    

If an idea no longer improves the portfolio:

- It is removed
    
- Archived
    
- Disabled
    

No debate.

---

## Why “It Still Makes Sense” Is a Red Flag

Anton warns:

> Logic can remain correct while the trade stops working.

Reasons:

- Others exploit the same logic
    
- Structural frictions vanish
    
- Time horizons mismatch
    
- Capital overwhelms the edge
    

Markets reward **timely edges**, not correct explanations.

---

## Archiving vs Forcing Ideas

Professionals do **not** delete ideas emotionally.

They:

- Archive them
    
- Label regimes where they worked
    
- Record failure conditions
    
- Wait for environments where they may return
    

Retail traders:

- Force ideas back into the market
    
- Resize them aggressively
    
- “Give them one more chance”
    

Archiving preserves knowledge **without risking capital**.

---

## The Cost of Forcing Dead Ideas

Anton emphasizes the real danger:

> Forcing dead ideas consumes risk budget that could be used elsewhere.

Consequences:

- Opportunity cost
    
- Increased correlation
    
- Portfolio drag
    
- Psychological erosion
    
- Cascading errors
    

Most blow-ups involve:

- Old ideas
    
- Over-sized positions
    
- Refusal to disengage
    

---

## Why This Must Be Mechanical

As with earlier lectures, Anton reinforces:

> Humans are terrible at letting go.

Therefore:

- Idea review must be systematic
    
- Performance thresholds must exist
    
- Disablement must be automatic
    
- Ego must be excluded
    

If a trader must _decide_ to let go, they usually won’t.

---

## Idea Lifecycle (Professional View)

Anton implicitly defines an idea lifecycle:

1. Hypothesis
    
2. Validation
    
3. Execution
    
4. Monitoring
    
5. Degradation
    
6. Disablement
    
7. Archive
    

Most traders stop at step 4.

Professionals plan for step 6 from the start.

---

## Key Takeaways (Part 7)

- All edges decay
    
- Attachment is inevitable — unless designed against
    
- Logic can persist while edge disappears
    
- Early degradation matters more than failure
    
- Ideas must earn capital continuously
    
- Archiving beats forcing
    
- Mechanical disablement prevents ruin

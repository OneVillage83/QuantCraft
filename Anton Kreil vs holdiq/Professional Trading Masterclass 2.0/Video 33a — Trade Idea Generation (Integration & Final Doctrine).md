## The Only Three Questions That Ever Matter

Anton reduces the entire idea process to three non-negotiable questions:

### 1. **Why does this idea exist?**

- What structural force creates it?
    
- What constraint prevents immediate arbitrage?
    
- Why now, not always?
    

If you cannot answer this cleanly, the idea is noise.

---

### 2. **Under what conditions does this idea stop working?**

- What regime invalidates it?
    
- What environment disables it?
    
- What signal says “this is no longer allowed”?
    

If there is no off-switch, the idea is dangerous.

---

### 3. **What happens if I am wrong?**

- How much risk does it consume?
    
- What does it do to the portfolio?
    
- Can it be survived without damage?
    

If being wrong breaks the system, the idea is rejected — even if it’s right most of the time.

## Why Ideas Must Be Boring to Be Professional

Anton reinforces something uncomfortable:

> **If an idea feels exciting, it is probably already crowded.**

Professional ideas are:

- Unexciting
    
- Conditional
    
- Size-constrained
    
- Slow to deploy
    
- Easy to abandon
    

Excitement correlates with ego.  
Ego correlates with rule-breaking.

## Why This Must Be Mechanical, Not Inspirational

Anton explicitly rejects motivation-based discipline:

> **You cannot rely on willpower under uncertainty.**

Therefore:

- Filters must be hard
    
- Gates must be enforced
    
- Ideas must be frozen
    
- Regime rules must override opinion
    

Professional trading removes _choice_ where choice is dangerous.

## The Trade Idea Generation Lifecycle (Final Form)

Anton implicitly locks the lifecycle as:

1. Idea emerges
    
2. Structural edge identified
    
3. Regime compatibility defined
    
4. Risk impact bounded
    
5. Portfolio role specified
    
6. Idea frozen
    
7. Execution permitted or denied
    
8. Performance monitored
    
9. Decay detected
    
10. Idea disabled
    
11. Idea archived
    

Anything missing → no trade.

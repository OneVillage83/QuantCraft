## Why “One More Improvement” Is the Start of Failure

Anton names the final trap:

> _“I understand the system now — I can improve it.”_

This leads to:

- Rule reinterpretation
    
- New exceptions
    
- Parameter tuning
    
- Edge resurrection
    
- Clever fixes
    

Every one of these feels justified.

Collectively, they **destroy the original logic**.

---

## The Professional Rule: Systems Must Be Hard to Change

Anton emphasizes:

> **If a system is easy to modify, it will be modified incorrectly.**

Professionals design systems where:

- Changes are rare
    
- Changes are slow
    
- Changes are documented
    
- Changes require justification
    
- Old versions are preserved
    

This prevents impulsive evolution.

---

## Why Strategy “Evolution” Is Usually Degeneration

Anton draws a sharp distinction:

- **Evolution**: slow, evidence-based, conservative
    
- **Degeneration**: reactive, emotional, performance-driven
    

Most traders think they are evolving.  
They are actually **responding to recent pain or recent success**.

Markets punish reactive systems.

## What This Lecture Is Really About

Anton is answering the operational question:

> **“If humans cannot self-regulate, what exactly replaces self-control?”**

The answer is **mechanical constraint**.

PRM is not guidance.  
It is **enforcement**.

---

## The Central Principle of PRM

Anton states the professional axiom clearly:

> **Risk must decrease as emotional pressure increases.**

Retail traders do the opposite:

- Lose → increase risk
    
- Win → increase risk
    

PRM is designed to **invert this instinct automatically**.

---

## Loss of Privilege: The Core PRM Mechanism

Anton introduces the most important PRM concept:

> **Trading is a privilege, not a right.**

Privileges are:

- Conditional
    
- Revocable
    
- Gradual
    

In professional environments:

- Traders do not “blow up”
    
- They are **throttled**
    
- Then **restricted**
    
- Then **removed** if necessary
    

PRM exists to prevent catastrophic failure by **progressive restriction**.

---

## Why Binary Stops Fail

Retail risk management often relies on:

- Hard stop losses
    
- Daily max loss
    
- Account kill switches
    

Anton explains why this is insufficient:

- Humans change behavior _before_ stops are hit
    
- Escalation happens below limits
    
- Stress builds invisibly
    
- Damage accumulates before “hard stops” trigger
    

PRM must intervene **before** catastrophe.

---

## Progressive Risk Throttling

Anton outlines the professional sequence:

As conditions deteriorate (losses, volatility, behavior signals):

1. **Reduce position size**
    
2. **Reduce gross exposure**
    
3. **Reduce trade frequency**
    
4. **Restrict eligible strategies**
    
5. **Force inactivity**
    
6. **Remove trading permission entirely**
    

This progression:

- Preserves capital
    
- Preserves psychology
    
- Preserves optionality
    

Retail traders skip straight from _full risk_ to _blow-up_.

---

## PRM Applies After Wins Too (Critical)

Anton emphasizes an often-missed point:

> **PRM must also activate after success.**

After winning streaks:

- Confidence increases
    
- Skepticism drops
    
- Rules feel unnecessary
    

Professional systems respond by:

- Freezing risk budgets
    
- Increasing scrutiny
    
- Preventing size escalation
    
- Blocking “earned aggression”
    

Success is treated as a **risk event**, not a reward.

---

## Drawdowns Change the Rules

Anton formalizes a professional standard:

> **Drawdown state determines which rules apply.**

As drawdown deepens:

- Allowed risk shrinks
    
- Allowed strategies narrow
    
- Allowed discretion disappears
    

This prevents:

- Revenge trading
    
- Doubling down
    
- Emotional escalation
    

Rules must change **with psychological state**, not with opinion.

---

## Why PRM Must Be Non-Negotiable

Anton is explicit:

> **If PRM rules can be overridden, they will be — eventually.**

Professional systems assume:

- Traders will argue
    
- Traders will rationalize
    
- Traders will feel “different”
    

Therefore:

- PRM overrides are forbidden
    
- Appeals are slow and external
    
- Changes require cooling-off periods
    

Delay is a feature, not a bug.

---

## PRM as a Behavioral Circuit Breaker

Anton reframes PRM’s function:

> **PRM breaks feedback loops before they cascade.**

Without PRM:

- Loss → stress → risk increase → bigger loss
    
- Win → confidence → risk increase → regime shift → collapse
    

With PRM:

- Feedback loops are interrupted
    
- Risk contracts automatically
    
- Time is forced back into the system
    

Time is the most powerful stabilizer.

---

## Why Professionals Accept Forced Inactivity

Anton highlights a key mindset shift:

> **Being forced to stop trading is not punishment.  
> It is protection.**

Institutions view inactivity as:

- Capital preservation
    
- Psychological reset
    
- System integrity maintenance
    

Retail traders view inactivity as:

- Failure
    
- Lost opportunity
    
- Personal inadequacy
    

This difference defines survival.

---

## Key Takeaways (PRM Part 2)

- Risk must fall as pressure rises
    
- Trading is a privilege, not a right
    
- Binary stops are insufficient
    
- Progressive throttling prevents catastrophe
    
- Success increases risk-taking
    
- Drawdowns change the rules
    
- PRM must be non-negotiable
